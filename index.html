A Delicate Balance

Coral reefs are often called the "rainforests of the ocean" for good reason. These vibrant ecosystems cover less than 1% of the ocean floor yet support roughly a quarter of all marine species.

But rising ocean temperatures and increasing acidity are disrupting the delicate chemistry that allows corals to build their calcium carbonate skeletons. The result: widespread bleaching events that leave reefs vulnerable and ecosystems in peril.

"Coral reefs support 25% of all marine life, yet cover less than 1% of the ocean floor."
— Coral Reef Alliance
